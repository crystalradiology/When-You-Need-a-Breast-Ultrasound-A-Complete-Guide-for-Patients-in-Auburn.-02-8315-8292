What Is a Breast Ultrasound?
A breast ultrasound is a diagnostic imaging procedure that uses high-frequency sound waves to create real-time images of breast tissue. Unlike X-rays or CT scans, ultrasound does not use radiation, making it a safe option for many patients, including pregnant or breastfeeding women.
During the procedure:
•	A trained sonographer applies a small amount of gel to the skin.
•	A handheld device called a transducer moves over the breast area.
•	Sound waves travel through the tissue and create images on a monitor.
Why Is a Breast Ultrasound Performed?
A breast ultrasound is typically not used as a routine screening tool for breast cancer. Instead, it is used when doctors need additional information after a physical exam or mammogram.
Here are the most common reasons a physician may recommend this scan.
1. To Investigate a Lump or Breast Abnormality
If a lump is discovered during a clinical breast exam or on a mammogram, an ultrasound can help determine whether it is:
•	A fluid-filled cyst (usually benign), or
•	A solid mass that may require further testing.
•	This distinction is extremely important in determining the next steps for diagnosis and treatment.
Many patients are referred for a Breast Ultrasound at Crystal Radiology to clarify findings and provide reassurance.
2. To Examine Dense Breast Tissue
Some women have dense breast tissue, which means there is more glandular and connective tissue than fatty tissue.
•	Dense breasts can make it harder for mammograms to detect abnormalities. Ultrasound helps by providing clearer imaging of dense tissue, allowing doctors to identify issues that might otherwise be difficult to see.
3. To Guide a Breast Biopsy
If doctors identify a suspicious area within the breast, a biopsy may be required to collect a small tissue sample.
•	Ultrasound imaging helps guide the needle precisely to the correct location, ensuring the most accurate sample is taken. This greatly improves the accuracy and efficiency of the biopsy procedure.
At Crystal Radiology, specialists use advanced imaging technology to ensure precise and safe biopsy guidance.
4. To Evaluate Breast Pain or Changes
Persistent breast pain, nipple discharge, or visible changes in the breast may indicate an underlying condition that requires further evaluation.
A breast ultrasound can help doctors examine:
•	Unexplained breast pain
•	Nipple discharge
•	Skin changes or swelling
•	Localised tenderness
These symptoms are not always serious, but imaging helps identify the cause and rule out potential concerns.
5. To Monitor Benign Breast Conditions
Some women develop benign breast lumps such as cysts or fibroadenomas. Although these are usually harmless, doctors often monitor them over time to ensure they remain stable.
Ultrasound is ideal for follow-up imaging because:
•	It does not expose patients to radiation
•	It provides clear and repeatable imaging
•	It allows doctors to track changes over time
Patients often undergo Breast Ultrasound at Crystal Radiology as part of ongoing breast health monitoring.
6. To Examine Breast Implants
Breast ultrasound can also evaluate the condition of breast implants, particularly silicone implants.
It helps doctors detect:
•	Implant ruptures
•	Leakage
•	Surrounding tissue abnormalities
Ultrasound imaging provides valuable insights into implant integrity and surrounding breast tissue health.
Who Should Consider a Breast Ultrasound?
Breast ultrasound is particularly beneficial for patients who require additional imaging or have symptoms that need further evaluation.
You may benefit from a breast ultrasound if you:
•	Have discovered a new breast lump
•	Have dense breast tissue
•	Are you pregnant or breastfeeding
•	Experience breast pain or nipple discharge
•	Have abnormal mammogram results
•	Have breast implants
•	Need imaging to guide a biopsy

Common Conditions Detected Through Breast Ultrasound
A breast ultrasound can identify many breast conditions, ranging from benign abnormalities to potentially serious concerns.
Some of the most commonly detected conditions include:
1. Breast Cysts
Fluid-filled sacs that are usually non-cancerous
Appear as dark, round, or oval structures on ultrasound images
2. Fibroadenomas
Solid benign tumors often found in younger women
Typically have smooth, well-defined edges on ultrasound scans
3. Breast Infections or Abscesses
Conditions like mastitis can cause swelling, inflammation, and fluid buildup
Ultrasound helps detect and assess the affected area
4. Lipomas
Soft fatty lumps under the skin
Generally harmless and rarely require treatment
5. Tumors (Benign or Malignant)
Ultrasound helps distinguish suspicious masses from benign growths
If necessary, a biopsy may be recommended for confirmation
6. Enlarged Lymph Nodes
Swollen lymph nodes near the breast may indicate infection, inflammation, or cancer spread
